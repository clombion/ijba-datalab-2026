### **Strategic & Pedagogical Introduction**

**The Strategic Approach: "Tabular Thinking" & The Horizon Table**
The 2025 curriculum builds on the standard Data Pipeline but introduces two critical strategic shifts early in the process:
*   **"Tabular Thinking" (La pensée tabulaire):** From Session 1, students are trained to **reverse-engineer** existing visualizations. By looking at a complex chart (e.g., car accidents by engine power), they must mentally reconstruct the spreadsheet (rows/variables) that generated it. This trains the "data eye" before they even touch a dataset.
*   **The Horizon Table (Tableau d'horizon):** The course explicitly moves away from "finding data and seeing what's inside." Instead, it enforces a **Design-First** approach. Students must formulate a hypothesis and design the empty table (the "Horizon Table") with the specific columns they need *before* they start searching for data.

**The Pedagogical Strategy**
*   **Deconstruction:** The course uses "reverse engineering" exercises (turning charts back into data tables) to demystify data structures.
*   **Structured Skepticism:** It frames data not as facts, but as "structured representations of the world" that can be prone to hallucinations or poor encoding.
*   **Tooling:** The stack remains accessible, centered on **Google Sheets** for logic, **Tabula** for unlocking PDFs, and **Pivot Tables** for analysis.

### **Course Outline (5 Sessions)**

#### **Session 1: Introduction & Data Logic**
*Focus: Defining data journalism, "Tabular Thinking," and formulating hypotheses.*

*   **The Ecosystem:**
    *   **Definition:** Informing the public by exploiting large volumes of structured data.
    *   **History & Examples:** From the Guardian's Datablog to "Chicago Crime" and "Swiss Leaks".
    *   **Applications:** Deciphering complex systems (e.g., French electrical grid) vs. personalized info (e.g., "Compare maternity wards near you").
*   **Core Concept: "La Pensée Tabulaire" (Tabular Thinking):**
    *   **Exercise:** Analyzing published charts (Libération, Le Monde) and reconstructing the underlying dataset: "What are the rows? What are the variables?".
*   **Methodology:**
    *   **The Hypothesis:** Moving from a general question (e.g., "Diversity in journalism schools") to measurable variables.
    *   **The Horizon Table:** Designing the target dataset (Rows = Observations, Columns = Variables) before finding data.

#### **Session 2: Finding & Acquiring Data**
*Focus: Sourcing strategies, scraping, and understanding data nature.*

*   **Sourcing Strategy:**
    *   **Locations:** Open Data portals (Data.gouv.fr), APIs, internal archives, or "Nowhere" (requiring original collection).
    *   **Formats:** Understanding the hierarchy from machine-readable (CSV, JSON, XLS) to difficult formats (PDF, Paper).
*   **Acquisition Techniques:**
    *   **Web Scraping:** Using **X-Ray Goggles** to understand HTML structure and **`IMPORTHTML`** in Google Sheets to pull tables directly from Wikipedia.
    *   **Manual vs. Auto:** Distinguishing between simple copy-paste and automated extraction tools like **Import.io** or **Octoparse**.
*   **Data Literacy:**
    *   **Concept:** Understanding that data is a "structured representation of the world," not the absolute truth.
    *   **Exercise:** "Press Subsidies" dataset—navigating raw data to answer specific questions.

#### **Session 3: Verification & Cleaning**
*Focus: Reliability, PDF extraction, and structural hygiene.*

*   **Verification (The 3 Stakes):**
    *   **Reliability:** Does the data represent reality? (e.g., checking for anomalies).
    *   **Exhaustiveness:** Is it complete? (e.g., identifying missing acts in medical data).
    *   **Quality:** Is it properly encoded?.
    *   **Case Study:** The "Maternity Wards" investigation by *Le Monde*, highlighting how to handle data with missing medical codes.
*   **The "Dirty" Reality:**
    *   Identifying common errors: Ambiguous null values (0 vs. empty), default dates (1900, 1970), and duplicates.
*   **Cleaning Tools & Strategy:**
    *   **PDF Extraction:** Using **Tabula** to liberate data locked in PDF files.
    *   **Grooming (Toilettage):** Using `SUBSTITUTE` to remove artifacts, `SPLIT` to separate units from values, and `UNIQUE` to find duplicates.

#### **Session 4: Consolidation & Analysis**
*Focus: Merging datasets and executing the analysis.*

*   **Consolidation:**
    *   **Rapprochement (Reconciliation):** Merging two separate datasets (e.g., cleaning results + new subsidy data) using a common key.
    *   **Tools:** Using `FILTER` and `INDEX/MATCH` to join tables.
*   **Analytical Methodology:**
    *   **Deconstruction:** Breaking a theme ("Rurbanization impact on digital divide") into a hypothesis ("Urban areas have more support centers"), then into a data question ("Count centers by INSEE code").
    *   **Case Study:** Analyzing the 2024 Legislative Elections. Question: "Which political nuances are the most gender-balanced?".
*   **Execution:**
    *   Using **Pivot Tables** and functions like `COUNTA` to calculate ratios and test the hypothesis.

#### **Session 5: Presentation**
*Focus: Storytelling and the limits of visualization.*

*   **Presentation Philosophy:**
    *   **Presentation vs. Visualization:** "Visualizing is not an end in itself." Sometimes the data is just a step in the investigation and doesn't need a chart.
    *   **Selection:** In a major investigation (e.g., "Conflicts of interest in hospitals"), only a fraction of the articles may need visualization.
*   **The Triad:** Balancing **Data** (Simple viz), **Form** (Attracting the eye), and **Message** (Storytelling).
*   **Tools:** Overview of **Datawrapper**, **Flourish**, and **RawGraph**.
