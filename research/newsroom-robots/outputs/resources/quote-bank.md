# Quote Bank: AI in Journalism

60 curated quotes from the Newsroom Robots podcast (2024-2026), organized by topic. Suitable for slides, readings, and classroom discussion prompts.

---

## THE VALUE OF JOURNALISM IN THE AI ERA

> "We're not in the business of saving journalists. We're not necessarily even in the business of saving journalism. We're in the business of saving information in the public interest."
> -- **Gina Chua**, Semafor, June 2025

> "If we still think that we're valuable only because we make this thing called content, we are doomed. And AI is not the agent of doom, but it shows us that future."
> -- **Jeff Jarvis**, CUNY, February 2024

> "If we imagine that reporting today is some mixture of reporting out facts... and then there's synthesizing and writing up of that new information, I think it does stand to reason that pressure AI puts on publishers means that there's more value in the former, in reporting new facts, than in the latter, writing them up."
> -- **Zach Seward**, New York Times, May 2025

> "We are now in a place, in a position, in a time, you call it an opportunity or you can call it a rolling disaster, whichever you want to call it. But the world is going to change... If we're going to reinvent journalism, let's burn it to the studs and think about what we should do and can do and who we can truly serve."
> -- **Gina Chua**, Semafor, June 2025

> "I started in journalism 25 years ago and I still write my stories the exactly the same way. So the article will die, should die, storytelling won't die."
> -- **Gard Steiro**, VG/Schibsted, July 2025

> "I don't think the question should be like, how do we save the article or how do we drive consumption on site. I think the question should be, what is the job that journalism needs to do for people and how can AI help us deliver that in new ways?"
> -- **David Chivers**, Lenfest AI Collaborative, December 2025

## WHAT AI CAN AND CANNOT DO

> "It was very popular to say that AI will replace all the boring tasks and we're gonna save so much time and money... But for us at Aftonbladet, at least for us, there aren't that many tasks that this technology can manage to automate. And we've been trying a lot, but we haven't really found that."
> -- **Martin Schori**, Aftonbladet, September 2024

> "A lot of the most interesting applications have nothing to do with writing articles or writing out creating prose at all, so much as creating structure out of unstructured data sets to help unlock stories that are hidden in massive data sets you probably previously couldn't have considered as possible to report on."
> -- **Zach Seward**, New York Times, May 2025

> "The more experience they had, the more useful the assistant was because what the assistant does is just the summaries and all the critical thinking and the nose for the news, we leave it up to the reporter."
> -- **Jaemark Tordecilla**, PCIJ/Philippines, June 2024

> "It's gone from spending an hour to an hour and a half sitting and manually doing this work to spending five minutes doing it... every day when you come to work, you have the day's stories ready for you. You just have to start calling sources."
> -- **Lars Adrian Giske**, iTromsoe, February 2025

> "We have often, coming from a tabloid background, we put a lot of prestige and pride in our headline making. That was one of the first things I heard about AI -- you can use it for headline generation. We've tried that a lot. But still, an editor will come up with an eleventh version that's even better than the first ten."
> -- **Martin Schori**, Aftonbladet, September 2024

> "An AI system cannot understand the vulnerability and pain of somebody who has just lost their house because of a bad discriminatory decision. Cannot understand what it feels like to be in the room as your son or daughter gets sentenced by an AI system for something they didn't do. Can't understand empathy or care, responsibility, or love. These are things that are uniquely human in this world."
> -- **Vilas Dhar**, Patrick J. McGovern Foundation, October 2025

## TRUST AND TRANSPARENCY

> "Fully automating AI applications are probably the best way to destroy trust in the media industry."
> -- **Alessandro Alviani**, Ippen Digital, February 2024

> "People are not in doubt that we are competent. They are in doubt about whether we want to do them well. Whether we really have their interests at our hearts."
> -- **Naja Nielsen**, Schibsted, July 2025

> "People don't come to the BBC News app or the website to get AI, right? You go there to get credible news... For us it's about how can we give people a better news experience, not an AI experience."
> -- **Olle Zacharison**, BBC, November 2025

> "Being transparent about what parameter you use is very nice and good, but it starts only being really meaningful if you also give people a choice."
> -- **Natali Helberger**, University of Amsterdam, July 2024

> "We didn't say AI was disqualifying in any fashion... But we also didn't linger on essentially a moral judgment about it. We just really believed that transparency is a value we should live by."
> -- **Neil Brown**, Pulitzer Prizes, February 2025

> "I'm not a big fan of the argument that almost every content already has pieces of AI in it or consists of is already based on several AI workflows, so you don't need to flag it out. I think the opposite is right. You need as quality media outlets, you're deeply depending on the trust of your users and especially your subscribers."
> -- **Fabian Heckenberger**, Suddeutsche Zeitung, January 2026

> "Transparency is the antiseptic."
> -- **Congresswoman Anna Eshoo**, US Congress, August 2024

## ETHICS AND RESPONSIBILITY

> "I feel like the risk with putting too strict guidelines is to communicate to your team that you don't trust them to know how to do the right thing."
> -- **Mattia Peretti**, JournalismAI, May 2024

> "I am very rarely optimistic about AI. But I am very optimistic about well-intentioned, values-driven people that are making decisions about how we use AI."
> -- **Vilas Dhar**, Patrick J. McGovern Foundation, October 2025

> "There's so much talk about, oh well, these AI LLMs, they are biased. And now it's actually an LLM that's helping us discover our biases. I think that's quite beautiful."
> -- **Ole Reissmann**, Der Spiegel, June 2024

> "We're spending a lot of time ensuring that the role of generative AI specifically is that there are guardrails around it and that we are not in the business of generating content at any point, up to and including links."
> -- **Aron Pilhofer**, Chicago Public Media, January 2026

> "Maybe the most obvious use cases of generative AI are not the ones we should go after. Because first we have to ask ourselves why we would want to use AI and what AI is actually good at."
> -- **Ole Reissmann**, Der Spiegel, June 2024

> "I really thought about how do I teach AI to see AI more than just a tool? It's a sort of like a socio-technical phenomenon that needs obviously more critical engagement."
> -- **Djordje Padejski**, Stanford University, September 2025

## LEADERSHIP AND ORGANIZATIONAL CHANGE

> "I think my most important task as a leader is not to point direction, but to foster a discussion within the organization."
> -- **Gard Steiro**, VG/Schibsted, July 2025

> "Even if the technology develops exponentially, then rolling it out and adapting it, that's change management. That's a very linear process."
> -- **Kasper Lindskow**, JP/Politikens, March 2025

> "Just because it's an idea doesn't mean it's a good idea. Kill this idea in its sleep, basically."
> -- **Olle Zacharison**, BBC, November 2025

> "Stop everything, allocate all resources to this project that Jacob is working on because it's like it's magic. It works."
> -- **Zetland journalist** (quoted by Tav Klitgaard), Zetland, December 2025

> "There is no done column when we build AI products, you know? There's no finish. We basically -- it's continuous learning, it's continuous monitoring."
> -- **Alessandro Alviani**, Suddeutsche Zeitung, January 2026

> "My mantra is think big, start small, move fast, learn fast."
> -- **Markus Franz**, Ippen Digital, November 2025

> "One of the opportunities that AI presents to us is better surfacing our humanity and better surfacing our authenticity... I think that this is a moment for us as leaders to reconsider that and to say we need to admit that we're humans and that we err."
> -- **Melissa Bell**, Chicago Public Media, January 2026

## AI LITERACY AND ADOPTION

> "If we don't know what we're talking about, if we don't know the possibilities, we will just miss the train and then realize that we have to use it but we don't know how and it's gonna be scary."
> -- **Florent Daudens**, Radio Canada, March 2024

> "We find that access to tools is really not the thing that unlocks people like converting to actual users. It's examples that resonate with them."
> -- **Zach Seward**, New York Times, May 2025

> "The number one mistake to avoid doing again is to put your head down and hope it goes away."
> -- **Neil Brown**, Pulitzer Prizes, February 2025

> "We're seeing more and more a two-tier approach inside of newsrooms... you have some people who are really, really interested in AI. And I would say maybe 80% of the people are like either they don't care or they don't have time or they're afraid of AI."
> -- **Florent Daudens**, Hugging Face/Radio Canada, July 2025

> "I cannot just teach them how to use NotebookLM today, but there's going to be a different NotebookLM that will be launched in five, ten years from now. And so how do I bring those fundamentals to them so they are ready to do this assessment on their own."
> -- **Djordje Padejski**, Stanford, September 2025

> "AI is not going to replace a journalist, right? But a journalist using AI might replace a journalist not using AI."
> -- **Djordje Padejski**, Stanford, September 2025

> "The more they got to use the tools, they realized this helps support me really well. And it also does a terrible job at my job if I leave it alone."
> -- **Matt Boggie**, Lenfest AI Collaborative, December 2025

## TECHNICAL ARCHITECTURE

> "I think it's all about combining complementary methods. Be they gen AI or machine learning or traditional just if-and-then coding. Once you start seeing how they can complement each other and building systems that integrate all of these approaches, that's when you get something really useful."
> -- **Lars Adrian Giske**, iTromsoe, February 2025

> "I do not trust the LLM and the probability model... I'm gonna put some authoritative sources in there and ask about what did the experts actually say."
> -- **Ole Reissmann**, Der Spiegel, June 2024

> "One of the key missions of Hugging Face is basically fighting the concentration of power. We want people to be able to choose how and what they want to use in AI."
> -- **Florent Daudens**, Radio Canada/Hugging Face, July 2025

> "I always compare it with the scribes and Gutenberg. So all of a sudden with Gutenberg, you had to put the scribes were faced with a machine that did most of what they almost all of what they did before, so they had to reinvent themselves."
> -- **Ludwig Siegele**, The Economist, September 2025

## POWER, PLATFORMS, AND ECONOMICS

> "Does the machine have the same right to read and learn and use information as a journalist? Because every journalist on Earth reads other journalists' work and gets information from sources and does not pay them, learns from it and uses it and repurposes it in their journalism."
> -- **Jeff Jarvis**, CUNY, January 2024

> "I think it's just very, very important that we're not naive about what are their goals. At the end of the day these are for-profit organizations. Their mission is profit. The mission is not the mission that we have as journalists."
> -- **Mattia Peretti**, JournalismAI, May 2024

> "If these model makers need fresh content, and of course traditional publishers won't be the only source of that, but if they want to have fresh content, they have to let us live. And good journalism is not cheap."
> -- **Ludwig Siegele**, The Economist, September 2025

> "In the future the enemy is not the publisher A or B. That's really clear. The enemy are the technology companies. So what we have to do is learn together on that journey, share experience."
> -- **Markus Franz**, Ippen Digital, November 2025

> "When an AI is ultimately going to do more reading than the humans, how do we monetize the AI's eyeball, essentially, is like an important question."
> -- **Sara Beykpour**, Particle, August 2025

> "There is no rich country with low energy. Similarly, there is no rich media company that doesn't have strong data pipelines, strong first-party data and deep algorithms."
> -- **Ritvvij Parrikh**, Times of India, October 2024

> "If we don't think about new business models for journalism in the AI shift, then the products can be as good as we hope for, but we will not be able to earn enough money to finance journalism."
> -- **Gard Steiro**, VG/Schibsted, July 2025

## INVESTIGATING AI AND ACCOUNTABILITY

> "AI systems are really unregulated by and large around the world. And so there's an opportunity to have real-world impact with journalism that deeply and in a nuanced fashion helps the public understand how these tools work, don't work and who they're impacting."
> -- **Garance Burke**, Associated Press, July 2024

> "Nobody else is as capable of asking the right questions in a methodical way... and verify the answers. And be able to very easily detect okay this is a hallucination because this information here is not part of the document because we are very good at verifying outputs."
> -- **Lars Adrian Giske**, iTromsoe, February 2025

> "We were able to identify moments in public meetings, like several years worth of public meetings where city county officials essentially proclaimed that they were not sharing data with ICE, even though they had just inked new contracts with ICE, like the day before."
> -- **Cheryl Phillips**, Stanford/Big Local News, May 2025

> "Journalism has a unique opportunity to share what it learns and fails at with AI deployment, building a repository of complex decisions that applies across all sectors."
> -- **Vilas Dhar**, Patrick J. McGovern Foundation, October 2025

## DATA PRIVACY AND SUSTAINABILITY

> "A good tip if you're going to build with infrastructure like this is call your lawyer first. That's like the golden rule of AI development and data journalism."
> -- **Lars Adrian Giske**, iTromsoe, March 2025

> "I think sometimes we also as an industry look at ourselves like I think sometimes we're too humble. We're working really with like high quality... for us it's not just GDPR, that's table stakes. It's also source protection. It cannot leak."
> -- **Tav Klitgaard**, Zetland, December 2025

> "If we were to deploy a user-facing product and potentially millions of people make use of these tools, that's for one costly. And then it's a question of, this compute, it doesn't come from nowhere, it costs energy, and is this a good use of energy right now."
> -- **Ole Reissmann**, Der Spiegel, June 2024

> "We should be asking, how do we transform our social practices so that we can leverage the ways we use these tools to minimize the queries we need, but get much better outcomes from them?"
> -- **Vilas Dhar**, Patrick J. McGovern Foundation, October 2025
