## Seek Help

If something goes wrong with your digital security, GIJN recommends reaching out to nonprofit groups dedicated to detecting and tracking attacks and training users:

- Access Now runs a 24/7 Digital Security Helpline available in nine languages: English, Spanish, French, German, Portuguese, Russian, Tagalog, Arabic, and Italian. They respond to all requests within two hours.
- The Committee to Protect Journalists (CPJ), based in New York, advocates on behalf of reporters around the world and fields requests for assistance. CPJ also has a digital safety toolkit with some excellent tips.
- Reporters Without Borders, based in Paris, does similar advocacy as CPJ. Reporters Without Borders runs an emergency assistance service for the media and a digital help desk to advise and support journalists on digital security. Find them at [helpdesk.rsf.org](https://www.helpdesk.rsf.org).
- The Citizen Lab at the University of Toronto, researches Internet security and human rights.

### ***Exercise 1: ChatGPT Plug-ins for Research***

ChatGPT plug-ins are add-ons you can pair with the AI chatbot to extend its capabilities. These are available through the ChatGPT plug-ins store, which we learned about in [Chapter 9](#ch09.xhtml__15031-5909_ch009). Only a few plug-ins are made by OpenAI itself, with the vast majority made by third-party developers. The plug-ins are free, but they're available only for ChatGPT Plus subscribers (\$20 monthly) through the ChatGPT store.

To access the plug-ins, click on the three dots in the lower left corner of the interface, go to Settings and make sure the Plug-ins option is turned on (green).

Then click on the GPT-4 button at the top of the ChatGPT interface, and make sure the plug-ins option is checked (it may default to it). A small pulldown menu and icons may appear underneath the GPT-4 interface, giving you access to the GPT store in the pulldown menu ([Figure 11.1](#ch11.xhtml__15031-5909_ch011_Figure_001)). You can install as many plug-ins as you like, but you can activate only up to three at a time.

### ***Exercise 2: Scraping Data from Google Finance***

::: section
#### *Reference Videos*

If you struggle with the step-by-step process in this exercise, just follow along with this Google Finance training video (<https://bit.ly/financetrain>) to learn how to scrape real-time and historical stock data into a Google Sheet. Business reporters use this to track companies and their stock prices across multiple markets.

::: section
#### *Getting Started*

Start by opening this page: [www.google.com/finance](http://www.google.com)

Then open this practice spreadsheet, then make a copy of it: <https://bit.ly/scrapefinance>

It's very important that you make a copy of the spreadsheet. Just go to the File: menu in the upper left corner and select "Make a Copy," then label it as you see fit.

Here's a quick list of Google Finance scraping formulas that you can use for later reference: [https://support.google.com/docs/answer/3093281?hl=en](https://support.google.com)

1.  **How to pull stock data into a spreadsheet:**
    - Go to the first tab of your copied spreadsheet. Tabs are at the bottom left of the sheet.
    - In cell b2, type: =googlefinance(A2,b1) and hit return for the price
    - In cell c2, type: =googlefinance(A2,c1) and hit return for the price-earnings ratio
    - In cell d2, type: =googlefinance(A2,d1) and hit return for the 52-week high price
    - Write your own script for a 52-week low column (E) by tweaking the aforementioned formulas (hint: e1)
    - The formula is pulling from the stock symbols in column 1 and the stock attributes (price, etc.) in the header row 1.
2.  **How to scrape historical stock prices:**
    - Paste or type: =GOOGLEFINANCE(a4, "price","03/10/2020", today(), "daily") in cell E1 to get daily ending prices since March 2020
    - GOO
    - Paste or type: =GOOGLEFINANCE(a3, "price","12/09/2020", today(), "weekly") in cell L1 to get end-of-week prices since March 2020
    - =GOOGLEFINANCE(a2, "price","05/06/2020", today(), "weekly")
    - Paste or type: =GOOGLEFINANCE("SSE", "price", DATE(2021,1,1), DATE(2022,05,06), "Weekly") in cell N1 to get end-of-week prices for the SSE since January 2021
    - Paste or type: =GOOGLEFINANCE("HSI", "price", DATE(2022,1,1), DATE(2022,07,26), "daily") in cell N1 to get end-of-day prices for the HSI since January 2022
    - Last 60 days of Tesla prices, paste or type:
    - =GOOGLEFINANCE("NASDAQ:TSLA","price",TODAY()-60, TODAY())
    - These functions will auto-update over time so you'll see current prices. It's similar to how data scraping works.
3.  **Focusing a sheet onto one stock**
    - Set up a sheet to look like this ([Figure 11.6](#ch11.xhtml__15031-5909_ch011_Figure_006)), then in cell B2 under Value, paste this and hit return:
      - =GOOGLEFINANCE("NASDAQ:GOOG",A2)
      - =GOOGLEFINANCE("HOKCY",A2)
      - =GOOGLEFINANCE("AAPL",A2)
      - =GOOGLEFINANCE("NYSE:f",A2)
      - =GOOGLEFINANCE("NYSE:vz",A2)
      - =GOOGLEFINANCE("XOM",A2)
      - =GOOGLEFINANCE("f",A2)
    - Then grab the blue square in the lower right of the cell and drag down: It will populate the sheet with data from Apple stock, and it'll update as the market changes.
4.  **Formulas:** To see any of the formulas, click on the blue? button on the cell when you type in =GOOGLEFINANCE. You also can see them when you hit return after typing in Google Finance and you can find many of them listed here: [https://support.google.com/docs/answer/3093281?hl=en](https://support.google.com)
5.  **Practice scraping some stocks with this formula:**
    - =GOOGLEFINANCE("STOCK SYMBOL",a1)
6.  **How to find stock ticker symbols:**
    1.  Type in the stock name into Google Finance and the symbol pops up.
    2.  Search the company name, exchange and the words stock symbol on Google
    3.  Go to the stock exchange's site and look for the database of them. Examples:
        - Stock Analysis: List of All Stock Ticker Symbols
        - NASDAQ Stock Screener: Database of Ticker Symbols
        - NYSE Listings Directory

### ***Exercise 3: Scraping a Website***

You can scrape data from the FDIC Failed Banks List (or any other site with a table of data) with this basic scraping formula. Need help? Watch this training video: <https://bit.ly/scrapingvid>

1.  Open a new Google Sheet by typing sheets.new into your browser field.
2.  Label your spreadsheet in the upper left: FDIC Failed Banks
3.  Paste this formula into Cell A1 of your Google Sheet:
    - =IMPORTHTML("[https://www.fdic.gov/resources/resolutions/bank-failures/failed-bank-list/](https://www.fdic.gov)","table",0)
    - Site: [https://www.fdic.gov/resources/resolutions/bank-failures/failed-bank-list/](https://www.fdic.gov)

Other sites to scrape (use the blank formulas that follow):

- [www.bls.gov/news.release/cpi.t02.htm](http://www.bls.gov)
- (CPI data from the Bureau of Labor Statistics)
- [www.geostat.ge/en/modules/categories/195/business-statistics](http://www.geostat.ge)
- (Georgia biz data)
- [www.inmo.ie/Trolley_Ward_Watch](http://www.inmo.ie)
- [www.transtats.bts.gov/AverageFare](http://www.transtats.bts.gov)

Just plug those web addresses into the URL field in the formulas below and paste them into cell A1 in your spreadsheet.

- =IMPORTHTML("URL','table",0)

### ***Exercise 4: Wolfram Alpha ChatGPT Plug-in***

It will solve math problems and do some basic almanac research for you. Here are a few prompts to get you started:

1.  First, ask it: What can I do with the Wolfram Alpha plug-in?

2.  Now ask it: What are examples of some prompts I can use with the Wolfram plug-in?

3.  Now give it a multilayered math problem:

    If my ads earn a CPM rate of \$30 for every 1,000 unique opens, and I have 10,000 unique people minimum for each post, how much can I expect to earn from the posts if I have two ads on each post?

4.  These are hit-and-miss, but give them a try:
    - How many over 60 live in Italy?
    - What is the gross domestic product of Guam over the past five years?
    - List the five cities in the world with the highest homicide rates.
    - Now list the five lowest.
    - Try some of your own prompts now that you have a feel for how to write them.

Summarize this writing and give me four actionable insights I can use as someone who \[your profession here ... e.g., reports stories about the economy\]

Write the complete code for a login page in Flutter. The design must be responsive and modern.

### ***Exercise 6: Flourish.studio -- Animated 3D Timeline Heatmap of Earthquakes Tectonic Plates***

What it could look like when you finish: [https://public.flourish.studio/visualisation/12816974](https://public.flourish.studio)

3.  Click on the Preview tab and drag the map out to the width of the screen, name it Earthquakes Animated Map in the upper left and fill in the boxes underneath with the numbers in [Figure 11.7](#ch11.xhtml__15031-5909_ch011_Figure_007): 1612 and 1000.

4.  In the Basemap tab on the right, select Flourish Dark (it should default to a vector map setting).

5.  Go to the Data tab at the top and click on the points tab and upload Earthquakes.csv.

6.  Then go to the right rail and fill in the fields to look like this ([Figure 11.8](#ch11.xhtml__15031-5909_ch011_Figure_008); delete C from start time if it's there).

7.  Now click on the Preview tab at the top and go to the Points menu on the right ([Figure 11.9](#ch11.xhtml__15031-5909_ch011_Figure_009)). Under Scale Type, elect numeric and hit the reverse button next to Palette.

8.  In the Palette pulldown, select Viridis.

9.  9\. Enter these settings:
    - In the Default Size field, type 2
    - In the Minimum field, type 1
    - In the Maximum field, type 20
    - In the Point Opacity field, type 75
    - In the Outline Mode bar, select Auto

10. Set Opacity underneath to 100 and width to 1.2.

11. Go to the Counter tab on the right side and hit the Disable button.

12. Go to the Data tab at the top, go to the right rail and add E next to Start Time field and hit return.

13. Now go back to the Preview Tab at the top, then click on the new Timeline tab that now appears on the right side and ...
    - For Visibility Height, use 4
    - Select Curved lines

14.  You can click on the button color and change it to gray if you want.

15. Set Timeline Duration to 16 or 17 seconds. You can make it slower if you want.

16. Under Aggregate Rate Interval, select Custom, then select Rate in the pulldown menu and months only for the rate ([Figure 11.10](#ch11.xhtml__15031-5909_ch011_Figure_010)).

17. Back to the Points tab on the right side, you can select from Pulse, Circles or Heatmap. Select Heatmap, and you can see patterns forming.

18. Now click on the Data tab at the top of the map. We're going to import the Tectonic Plates JSON file in our Google Drive.
    - Select the Lines tab at the top and upload the Tectonic Plates JSON file
    - Set the Geo column to A (if not a default)
    - Set the Series column to C
    - Then hit the Preview tab at the top
    - Hit the Lines tab on the right and adjust thickness and opacity of the lines to your preferences

19. Open the Header flyout on the right menu:

    Add this text to the Title Field: Earthquakes Worldwide (2000-Feb. 1, 2023)

20. Open the Footer flyout on the right menu and add:
